G - Moving Objects

  • Now, you will be adding a moveable physics object that will interact with your floor.

  • 1. (1) Be sure World is selected and click the + icon to add a child; (2) Search "rigidb" and (3) double click RigidBody from the list of nodes.

    A RigidBody is very similar to a StaticBody. The difference is that a RigidBody is intended to move, so it's automatically affected by gravity.

  • The RigidBody should have the familiar yellow caution symbol next to it. It's for the same reason as before with your StaticBody.

    You will carry out the same process as before, but I've added a video because it's a wordy process:

  • 2. Add a CollisionShape node and MeshInstance node as children of your RigidBody. Under the Inspector tab, add a BoxShape to the CollisionShape's Shape parameter, and a CubeMesh to the MeshInstance's Mesh parameter.

  • 3. With the RigidBody selected, click the green arrow and drag upwards to lift the RigidBody up.

  • 4. With the RigidBody still selected, click and drag a ring to rotate your RigidBody (I renamed mine to "Box").
