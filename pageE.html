E - The Inspector

  • 1. Again, you have a yellow caution symbol, warning you of something. With the CollisionShape selected, observe the Inspector tab on the right side of the screen.

    The Inspector contains parameters of the selected node. As the tooltip says while hovering the yellow caution symbol, The CollisionShape requires you to change its "Shape" parameter.

  • 2. In the Inspector tab, click the dropdown menu next to the Shape parameter. Then, click New BoxShape. The caution symbol should now disappear.

    Your StaticBody node named "Floor" can now detect collisions in our scene because you added Shape data to it.

  • 3. To make your Floor visible, add a MeshInstance node as a child to the Floor (StaticBody).

    Once again, be sure you (1) have the Floor (StaticBody) selected. (2) Search "meshin" to find and (3) select the MeshInstance node from the list (double click) to add a the desired child.

  • 4. Similarly to step 1, you must use the Inspector to modify a parameter. This time, you must modify the Mesh parameter of the newly added MeshInstance node. Click the dropdown menu and click New CubeMesh
